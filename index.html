Project Overview

MVP & Academic Validation

LithoQuest is a highly advanced, AI-driven data processing platform engineered specifically to revolutionize mineral discovery and complex geochemical analysis. By bridging the critical gap between raw, unstructured fieldwork data and actionable spatial intelligence, the platform dramatically accelerates decision-making and operational efficiency in the physical sciences.

At its core, the LithoQuest application utilizes a Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture to ingest and process complex geophysics metrics, such as Vertical Electrical Sounding (VES) parameters, groundwater exploration data, and mineral liberation metrics like lepidolite crushing requirements. The system automatically synthesizes these dense, unstructured geological reports into clean, highly structured JSON arrays, which are then embedded and stored within local vector databases.

Currently operating in its Minimum Viable Product (MVP) phase, LithoQuest is being developed and rigorously tested within a closed, academic environment by peer geosciences researchers at the University of Nigeria, Nsukka (UNN). The core platform relies on advanced Large Language Models serving strictly as internal reasoning engines for data extraction, severely constrained by technical system prompts to prevent hallucination.

By automating the tedious data-structuring phase of geological exploration, LithoQuest empowers geoscientists to focus on high-level spatial mapping and resource evaluation. Ultimately, the project aims to modernize the broader mining and geosciences sector, providing a scalable, secure, and highly accurate computational foundation for the next generation of sustainable mineral and groundwater exploration across West Africa and beyond.

The Industry Problem

Exploration is stuck in manual mode

The mineral exploration sector — particularly critical battery metal discovery like lithium from lepidolite — depends on tedious, error-prone manual processing that hasn't meaningfully changed in decades.

Geoscientists lose weeks to routine calculations. Stratigraphic knowledge sits in disconnected silos. And geology students graduate without ever touching the AI tools the modern industry demands.

The gap between fieldwork and actionable intelligence has never been wider. LithoQuest closes it.
